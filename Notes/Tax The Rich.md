## Another ranting RANT

## For all unrealized investment gains over a $10 million valuation

We’re talking about billionaire investors, not 401K retirement funds. No one intends on going after middle class retirement investments. This is about the multi-millionaire and billionaire class living off inflating their investment and corporate valuations while never returning anything to their business or workers. It’s about the über wealthy who often don’t feature as owners on the “investment” properties they call home — living lives of obscene luxury without paying taxes.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/0*nAYX2cDOuS6emsnC)

Right now, my husband and I will be paying more taxes on our house when the state revalues it. We paid $150K for it, but it’s rocketed up in value to $350K. Here’s the kicker: ==We cannot just sell and take those gains because everything else shot up in value too==. So, it will almost never be real gains for us. We will likely never realize any benefit from our home valuation.

But, those billionaire stock holders will use those stocks to valuate their companies and leverage that value for more wealth — without paying tax on any of it because they find ways to write off or evade taxes. Billionaires are essentially hiding their wealth in plain sight while continuing to realize the massive benefits of that wealth.

The arguments against taxing investment gains are the billionaires’ arguments. They want us to think a tax targeted narrowly to them will hurt us. But the truth is, higher taxes on them really does make them put money back into development and community and their employees.

==In 1941 to 1963, the tax rate was 91% or more on the wealthy, and 70% from 1965 to 81. In those years, we saw the greatest expansion of our middle class and a massive shrinking of poverty in this country. We also saw the greatest infrastructure building in our history.==

Since the tax rate on the already rich has been chopped back and chopped back, ad nauseam, since Reagan onwards, the middle class has been shrinking. And, poverty levels have been growing.

Because the billionaire and millionaire classes pay little to no taxes, the infrastructure they use to push their products have fallen into shameful decline. They hold 40% of the wealth of this country but number less than 10% of the population who are barely holding on. The rich expanded their combined wealth by two TRILLION dollars during a global pandemic while the rest of us languished.

That wealth was generated by us. The middle class and hard working poor make it rain dolla-dolla-bills, y’all; it rains right into the coffers of the already obscenely rich. And, the über rich reap the rewards, off of our labors.

Don’t let the corporatist billionaires tell you measures taxing them are aimed at you. They are not. Those massive tax breaks for the rich, though… those are aimed at the heart of America’s middle class and working poor.
