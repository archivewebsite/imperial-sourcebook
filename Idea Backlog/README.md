# Idea Backlog

This folder is the neutral parking lot for concepts, projects, and goals that are too good to forget, but not currently active. It keeps your immediate workspace clean while preserving your long-term inspiration.

## Usage Instructions

*   **Capture Freely:** Whenever you have a substantial idea (e.g., "Write a sci-fi novel", "Learn to speak Japanese", "Visit Iceland"), document it here as a new note.
*   **Preserve Project Focus:** By moving distant or undefined goals into this backlog, you ensure that your `Projects` folder contains strictly active, actionable work.
*   **Evaluate Periodically:** Review the contents of this backlog every few months (such as during a quarterly review). If an idea is ready to be acted upon, move it to your `Projects` folder and assign it a clear finish line.

## Tips for Maintaining the Backlog

### 1. Be Descriptive, Not Vague
Instead of just writing "Coding" as an idea, write "Build a personal portfolio website using React." The more specific the concept, the easier it is to evaluate later whether you actually want to commit to it.

### 2. Remove the Guilt of Delay
The primary purpose of this list is to relieve the pressure of having to do everything immediately. If an idea sits in this backlog for three years, that is completely fine—it simply means it hasn't become a priority yet.

### 3. Categorize by Estimated Effort
If the backlog becomes too lengthy or overwhelming, consider grouping ideas by their required effort level. You can separate "Quick Wins" (ideas achievable in a weekend) from "Life Goals" (multi-year endeavors) using tags or sub-folders.
