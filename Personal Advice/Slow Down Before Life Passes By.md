## The more you hurry, the more you miss what truly matters

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/0*NB5Z48ZI0jFChoyc)

Photo by Dewang Gupta on Unsplash

There are days when life feels unbearably loud.

Notifications buzzing. Deadlines piling up. People asking for things. Expectations sitting heavily on your chest.

You move from one task to another without even noticing how tired you are.

And somewhere in the middle of all that noise, you quietly wonder, “ *Is this what life is supposed to feel like?”*

We spend so much of our time trying to keep up.

Keep up with other people’s progress. Keep up with our own plans. Keep up with the version of ourselves we thought we would be by now. But life does not hurry the way we do. It unfolds gently, like morning light slowly filling a room.

Often, our suffering comes not from life itself, but from the way we resist its pace.

There was a time when I believed that if I just tried harder and worked longer, worried more and planned better, then everything would fall into place faster.

I thought rest was laziness.

I thought stillness meant falling behind.

But all that striving left me exhausted and strangely disconnected from the very life I was trying to build.

One afternoon, after an extremely overwhelming week, I sat alone with no music, no phone, no distractions.

At first, it felt uncomfortable.

My mind raced. I kept reaching for something to fill the silence. But after a while, the noise inside me softened. I noticed my breathing. I noticed the way sunlight touched the floor. And for the first time in a long while, I felt present.

Nothing in my external life had changed.

My responsibilities were still there. My uncertainties were still waiting. But something inside me shifted. I realised life is not something happening in the future, after everything is fixed.

It is happening now, in the quiet moments we rush past.

We are always chasing the next chapter, believing that happiness will finally settle when we arrive somewhere else: a better job, a healthier relationship, a clearer plan.

Yet when we get there, the mind quickly creates another destination. And so we keep running.

But what if this moment, imperfect as it is, is already enough?

Life is not asking you to become extraordinary overnight. It is asking you to be present. To notice the small mercies: a kind word, a warm drink, a brief laugh with someone you love. These are not insignificant. They are life itself.

Sometimes we feel lost because we are trying to control what cannot be controlled.

We want certainty. We want guarantees. We want to know that our efforts will be rewarded exactly as we imagine. But life rarely moves according to our script. And that is not a punishment.

It is an invitation to trust more deeply.

When we stop fighting every inconvenience, when we stop measuring ourselves against others, when we allow ourselves to simply be, something softens.

We become kinder. Not only to others, but to ourselves.

You don’t need to have everything figured out right now. You don’t need to be further ahead. You don’t need to rush your growth. The flower does not bloom by force. It blooms when it is ready.

If you are feeling overwhelmed, pause. Take one slow breath. Let your shoulders drop. Remind yourself that life is not a race you must win. It is a journey you are meant to experience.

When you slow down, life speaks.

And if you listen closely, it is not demanding perfection.

It is gently asking you to come home to this moment.
