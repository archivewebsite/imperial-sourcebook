Tags: Reality Check
Date: January 24, 2026

You are 30, everyone you know is moving so far ahead in life they are a speck. Most of your friends moved on. You have no one but your own insecurities to talk to. You used to be a risk-taker but now it's more "meh". If you are about to give up, this is for you.

For most of us, the story starts very young. And if you are someone living in the so-called "third world", the default setting is HARD MODE for you. You are automatically a part of a rat race whether you want to be in or not. In the age of playing around and figuring out what you want to do in life, you are taught what to chase in life. But even if you win the rat race, you are still a rat.

Then enters the age of survival. This is where my life took a pivot as I found something called cryptocurrencies. You may know it as that "internet scam thing" or a digital bubble. For me, it became a home.

A little background of who I am and where I come from. I grew up dreaming of becoming a cricketer, gave it up coz my dad couldn't pay for the academies I would have to join back then to even have a chance. I don't blame him, he did way more for me than he could afford to, and then some. I wasn't sure of what I will do in life, but one thing I was sure of was that I will never become a part of the rat race. I am not built for a conventional 9-5. I am built for a 24/7.

Then I started writing blogs, they became fairly popular (gonna keep em unnamed for privacy purposes) but I was missing an important aspect of it. Money. It was popular but for regular blogs, Adsense is not a viable source of income. One day, while doing some research for my blogs, I stumbled upon some crazy invention. Bitcoin.

It wasn't that trendy still, but enough to catch my attention. But there was one slight problem. I was poor and my family wasn't exactly wealthy either. I would be honest, I wasn't even sure if it was a scam or not but I have a bug in my ass and I always try out anything that intrigues me. I bought with whatever  funds I could arrange, it was barely anything, and in the great grand scheme of things it was nothing.

No one taught me anything about it because no one was there to teach me. And once again, I did what I always do: **fuck around and find out**.

And so I did. This was before I was Grinding Poet. The 2018 bear market hit me hard. My already tiny portfolio vanished. But my conviction in the space 100x'd. We had some savings, so I started **"DCA'ing"** whatever little I could every month throughout 2018 and 2019. Took a lame ass IRL job in 2020 and started putting 100% of my paycheck into crypto. Cut to November 2021, I did it. I made enough to retire me and my dad and I quit the job. I had made it, or so I thought.

Whatever that could go wrong, did. The trembles of 2022 were so violent, I could feel it even now. I have surpassed my 2021 NW multiple times since then, but every single time, life found a way to take it all away from me in a flash. I didn't lose it on gambling or leverage. I spent it to either buy life for my loved ones or a better life for some less fortunate strangers. And I don't regret a second of it. Such is life. You win some you learn some.

**But why am I telling you all this?**

If you read till now, it's either you are a lunatic or you have a similar story, or both. The plot could differ, the circumstances could be a 180 but the end result is the same. In your head, you are defeated. Whether by life, love or the markets, you are defeated. And now, you are finding it difficult to cope with the fact that someone less deserving than you is living the life you always dreamt of.

You did everything right and life still beat you. You are lost and unsure which path will lead you to glory. For you, it's all pointless. It was for me too. Trust me when I say this. I too felt envy of others' success at many-a-occasions.

It's natural and in times of desperation, it's understandable too. It's okay.

But what isn't okay is giving up. If you are alive, you haven't lost yet. Winning is a mindset. You gotta win your mind before you can win the world.

There are countless examples of people winning against all odds. That's you. Life works in mysterious ways, and it rewards those that don't give up.

*“I know of no better life purpose than to perish attempting the great and impossible.” ~* **Friedrich Nietzsche**

Why do you think the Penguin is trending? People resonate with it and many of them wish they were like that Penguin. It died some 20 years ago, but what didn't die was the thought of the penguin defying all norms and heading for what which it desired. Even if it meant death. You are someone who defied all norms and are doing something you desire. It could be trading random memes, building a spaceship, mowing your lawn. It doesn't matter what the "thing" is, what matters is the desire for it. Desires keep us human and alive. Without desires, we're just rats in the race.

You have to keep trying, not just for yourself, but everyone you love. You owe it to them. You owe it to your ancestors. You owe it to your bloodline.

I know for a fact that I am not giving up. I realized years ago the success I was made for and I won't stop until I am there. I'd either win or die trying.

So, I ask you again:
Do you still feel defeated? Or have you finally come to the conclusion that **until death, all defeat is psychological.**
