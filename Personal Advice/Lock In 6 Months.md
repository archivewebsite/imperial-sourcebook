# **Use this unknown example of locking in as a metaphor for your life**

The 1972 Olympics happened before I was born. Grandpa told me the story.

Lasse Virén was running the 10,000-meter final. Just another race. Nothing special. Just past the halfway mark, Virén got caught up with another runner, and they both fell to the ground. Runners rarely recover from a fall. Especially this far in.

The fall breaks your rhythm. Your adrenaline spikes. The runners at the front are so far ahead that they leave you in the dust. It’s typical after a fall to either quit the race or finish in last place, so you can do it for the honor.

Virén was a psycho. He did none of this normie sh*t.

Without questioning what happened, he got up within a few seconds. He didn’t sprint. He slowly made his way back to the main pack. After another 600 meters he’d made it back to the top 5.

This is when he locked in.

With 600 meters in the race left, Virén ran so aggressively it broke the spirit of the other runners. The look in his eyes was enough to scare away the devil himself.

This lock in won him the race. He also broke the world record. I challenge you to find another sporting example where someone has an accident then breaks the world record.

A few days later, Virén competed in the 5000m race and won that as well. The psychological reset Virén pulled off was so violent it changed running history.

This running story is a metaphor. You have likely had one or several falls over the last 10 years. You can either use those falls to lock in, or you can use them as the reason you won’t be locking in for the rest of 2026.
